Don't Carry Me Like Guilt

By: Deepesh
Jun 30, 2025 - 05:35

I won’t punish myself,

though your ache clings to my breath.

I won’t turn my fists inward,

for your wounds are not my debt.

You are not a burden,

you are not a curse.

You are thunder caught mid-cry,

a heart that learned to verse.

If I reached too close,

it was not to pierce,

but to shelter the fragile flame

flickering behind your fears.

I see the mirror too—

cracked and cruel and clear—

and still I stay,

not out of pity, but to hear.

To hear you.

To hold you in the silence

between your falling words.

To be the still in your storm,

the calm between the birds.

Yes, you are learning—

and I, too, am learning still.

That friendship isn't flawless,

and love sometimes hurts until...

Until it heals.

Until it forgives.

Until it simply is.

So I will not punish,

nor carry what is not mine.

But I’ll walk with you, gently,

along the maybe-line.

For your sorrow shaped me.

Your strength, it wakes me.

Your mirror shows me

a truth that remakes me.

So don’t silence your ache.

Don’t shrink from your name.

You are the friend I never sought—

and loved just the same.
